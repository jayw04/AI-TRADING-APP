Recommended Mean-Reversion Candidates
Priority	ID	Strategy	Main advantage
1	MR-002	Sector-Neutral Residual Reversion	Highest probability of producing a diversified, scalable edge
2	RNG-002	Regime-Gated Adaptive Range Reversion	Explicit range strategy with breakout protection
Short-term equity reversal has substantial historical evidence, including a maintained daily reversal factor in Kenneth French’s research library. However, research also indicates that much of the return behaves like compensation for providing liquidity, meaning the opportunity varies significantly by market conditions.

1. MR-002 — Sector-Neutral Residual Reversion
Hypothesis
After removing broad-market and sector movements, unusually large company-specific price moves frequently represent temporary liquidity pressure. Those residual moves should partially reverse over the following one to five trading days.
This differs from the rejected strategy:
	It does not fade price simply because it moved away from VWAP. 
	It removes market and sector trends before measuring overextension. 
	It creates many diversified trades rather than relying on a few range episodes. 
	It excludes likely information-driven moves such as earnings gaps. 
Residual statistical-arbitrage approaches have been studied using factor and principal-component models, while pairs-trading research has documented the broader relative-value mean-reversion mechanism. Those historical results do not guarantee that the edge remains profitable today, but they provide a stronger economic foundation than a standalone technical fade. 
Initial universe
Use a point-in-time universe consisting of approximately:
	Top 200–300 US common stocks by 60-day median dollar volume 
	Price above $10 
	Median daily dollar volume above $25 million 
	No ETFs, ADRs, preferred shares, SPACs, or recent IPOs 
	Short-borrow availability required for short positions 
	Exclude earnings and major corporate-event windows 
Starting with liquid large-cap stocks is important because high-turnover strategies can lose their apparent edge after realistic trading costs. Research on anomaly implementation finds that large-cap, high-turnover implementations are more likely to survive costs than small, illiquid-stock versions. 
Signal construction
For every stock, estimate the following rolling 60-session model:
r_(i,t)=α_i+β_(m,i) r_("SPY" ,t)+β_(s,i) r_("Sector" ,t)+ϵ_(i,t)

Where:
	r_(i,t)is the stock return. 
	r_("SPY" ,t)is the market return. 
	r_("Sector" ,t)is the corresponding sector ETF return. 
	ϵ_(i,t)is the company-specific residual. 
Then calculate:
	Cumulative residual return over the previous five sessions. 
	Rolling 60-day standard deviation of the five-day residual. 
	Residual z-score. 
z_(i,t)="Five-day cumulative residual" /"Rolling residual volatility" 

Entry rules
Long
Enter long when all are true:
	Residual z-score ≤ −2.0 
	Stock is within the bottom 10% of eligible residual scores 
	No earnings announcement from two sessions before through two sessions after entry 
	No merger, split, delisting, or major corporate-action flag 
	One-day opening gap is less than 6% 
	Bid-ask spread and estimated impact are within the approved liquidity envelope 
Short
Enter short when all are true:
	Residual z-score ≥ +2.0 
	Stock is within the top 10% of eligible residual scores 
	Same event and liquidity exclusions 
	Borrow is available 
	Estimated borrow cost is below the registered limit 
Exit rules
Close a position at the first occurrence of:
	Residual z-score returns inside ±0.35 
	Five trading sessions have elapsed 
	Residual moves farther to ±3.5 and the market or sector confirms the move 
	Earnings or another prohibited event enters the next-session window 
	Portfolio risk limit requires reduction 
Do not use a tight ordinary price stop. Mean-reversion trades often worsen temporarily before reverting. The stop should detect a likely hypothesis failure, not ordinary noise.
Portfolio construction
	Market beta approximately neutral 
	Sector exposure approximately neutral 
	Equal residual-risk contribution 
	Maximum position: 1.5% of gross exposure 
	Maximum sector gross exposure: 20% 
	Initial gross exposure: 100% 
	Initial portfolio volatility target: 8% 
	Increase gross exposure only after validation and live-paper evidence 
	No position should contribute more than 3% of projected portfolio risk 
Why this is the primary candidate
MR-002 has the strongest profit potential of the two because it obtains returns from:
	Many independent stock-level opportunities 
	Both long and short positions 
	Market and sector neutralization 
	Daily portfolio refresh 
	Controlled portfolio-level scaling 
The profit potential should come from breadth and repeatability, not from more leverage or wider entry thresholds.

2. RNG-002 — Regime-Gated Adaptive Range Reversion
Hypothesis
Highly liquid ETFs frequently oscillate within temporary, statistically stable ranges. A false break through a recently tested range boundary, followed by a close back inside the range, can identify a short-duration reversal.
The strategy must trade only when there is evidence of an established range. It must stop immediately when the range changes into a directional breakout.
Initial universe
Start with liquid ETFs rather than individual stocks:
	SPY 
	QQQ 
	IWM 
	DIA 
	XLK 
	XLF 
	XLE 
	XLV 
	XLI 
	XLP 
	XLY 
	XLU 
Use 30-minute bars during regular US trading hours.
ETFs reduce several problems that damaged the original approach:
	Individual-company earnings gaps 
	Corporate-event contamination 
	Difficult short-borrow conditions 
	Point-in-time constituent bias 
	Single-stock headline risk 
Range definition
For each ETF, use the preceding 60 completed 30-minute bars.
Calculate:
	Robust upper boundary: 90th percentile of closing prices 
	Robust lower boundary: 10th percentile 
	Centerline: median closing price 
	ATR over 20 bars 
	ADX over 14 bars 
	Linear-regression slope over 40 bars 
A valid range requires all of the following:
	ADX below 20 
	Absolute 40-bar slope below 0.10 ATR per bar 
	Range width between 1.5 and 5.0 ATR 
	At least two prior touches near both the upper and lower boundaries 
	No close more than 0.5 ATR beyond either boundary during the lookback 
	Current opening gap below 0.75 daily ATR 
This avoids trading both extremely narrow pre-breakout compression and markets that are already trending.
Long entry
Enter long after a lower-boundary false break:
	Current bar trades below the lower range boundary. 
	Current bar closes back above the lower boundary. 
	The close is in the upper 50% of the bar’s high-low range. 
	ADX remains below 22. 
	Volume is below 2.0 times its time-of-day median. 
	There is no simultaneous downside breakout in SPY unless the instrument is SPY itself. 
Enter at the next bar’s open.
Short entry
Use the inverse conditions:
	Price trades above the upper boundary. 
	The bar closes back inside the range. 
	Close is in the lower 50% of the bar. 
	Trend, volume, and market-confirmation vetoes remain satisfied. 
Exit rules
Take profit in two stages:
	Exit 60% at the range centerline. 
	Exit the remaining 40% at the opposite 75th or 25th percentile. 
Close the entire position when:
	Price closes 0.6 ATR outside the range 
	ADX rises above 25 
	Range slope exceeds its permitted limit 
	Eight bars have elapsed 
	The session reaches 3:45 p.m. Eastern Time 
No overnight positions in the initial version.
Position sizing
	Risk 0.20% of capital per trade 
	Maximum three simultaneous ETF positions 
	Reduce correlated exposure when multiple index or sector ETFs signal together 
	Combined SPY/QQQ/DIA/IWM risk capped at 0.35% 
	One re-entry per instrument per session 
	Initial portfolio volatility target: 6% 
Why this is different from RNG-001
RNG-002 is not another VWAP deviation fade.
RNG-001 problem	RNG-002 response
Price deviation treated as sufficient evidence	Requires an established, repeatedly tested range
VWAP provided the primary anchor	Uses robust rolling range boundaries
Weak breakout separation	Uses slope, ADX, range stability and false-break confirmation
Single entry around an apparent extreme	Requires price to break and then reclaim the boundary
Potential information-driven moves	Uses ETFs and rejects abnormal gaps and volume
Apparent edge failed clustered resampling	Must pass the same clustered test before consideration

Backtest Design
The strategy should not be optimized directly against the final test period.
Frozen parameter policy
Register only three configurations for each strategy:
MR-002
	Entry z-score: 1.75, 2.00, 2.25 
	Maximum holding period: fixed at five sessions 
	Exit z-score: fixed at 0.35 
RNG-002
	ADX ceiling: 18, 20, 22 
	Boundary percentiles: fixed at 10/90 
	Maximum holding period: fixed at eight bars 
Do not run hundreds of parameter combinations. The probability of selecting a false winner increases as more strategy configurations are tested. PBO and Deflated Sharpe methods were specifically developed to detect this form of backtest selection bias. 
Required testing sequence
	Development sample: first 50% of history. 
	Walk-forward validation: next 25%. 
	Sealed out-of-sample test: final 25%. 
	Block or date-clustered bootstrap. 
	Bull, bear, sideways, high-volatility and low-volatility decomposition. 
	Transaction-cost sensitivity. 
	Parameter-neighborhood stability. 
	Deflated Sharpe and PBO analysis. 
	Paper-trading verification before production. 
Transaction costs
Use observed spread and volume-based impact where possible.
Minimum stress tests:
Strategy	Base model	Stress model
MR-002	Spread + impact + borrow	2× spread/impact and 2× borrow
RNG-002	Spread + 1-bar slippage	2× spread and 2-bar adverse slippage
A short-horizon strategy that passes only with zero-cost or close-price execution must be rejected.

Proposed Pass Gates
A strategy passes only when all mandatory gates are satisfied:
Gate	Requirement
Net out-of-sample Sharpe	≥ 0.70
Net out-of-sample Calmar	≥ 0.75
Maximum drawdown	≤ 15%
Positive walk-forward folds	≥ 60%
Bootstrap mean-return lower bound	Above zero
Cost stress	Still profitable at 2× modeled costs
Parameter stability	Adjacent configurations remain profitable
PBO	Below 20%
Deflated Sharpe significance	At least 95%
Profit concentration	No single year contributes more than 35% of total P&L
Regime concentration	No single market regime contributes more than 60% of total P&L
Capacity	Positive after realistic volume participation limits
These are deliberately harder than selecting the highest historical CAGR. Technical-rule research shows why data-snooping adjustments are necessary when many variants have been examined. 
Final Recommendation
Open both records, but validate them in this order:
	MR-002 Sector-Neutral Residual Reversion — primary candidate 
	RNG-002 Regime-Gated Adaptive Range Reversion — secondary candidate 
MR-002 has the better chance of becoming a profitable standalone strategy because it has greater breadth, diversification and scalability. RNG-002 should initially be treated as a smaller complementary sleeve unless it independently passes every statistical and cost gate.
The governance disposition should be:
RNG-001 remains Completed · Rejected (Evidenced) · Archived. MR-002 and RNG-002 are new hypotheses, not continuations or parameter repairs of RNG-001.
That is the right next step:
Start with MR-002, then test RNG-002 separately. Before running either backtest, freeze:
	Universe and point-in-time data rules 
	Entry, exit, sizing, and exclusion rules 
	Transaction-cost and short-borrow assumptions 
	Development, validation, and sealed test periods 
	Pass/fail thresholds 
Do not adjust parameters after viewing the sealed out-of-sample results. Any change should create a new version, such as MR-002-v2, and require a new untouched test period.
For the first review, capture these results:
Area	Required output
Performance	CAGR, Sharpe, Sortino, Calmar, max drawdown
Trade quality	Win rate, payoff ratio, expectancy, trade count
Robustness	Walk-forward folds, bootstrap confidence interval, parameter neighbors
Costs	Gross versus net results, 1× and 2× cost stress
Stability	Annual and market-regime attribution
Concentration	Top trades, top dates, sectors, instruments
Capacity	Turnover, volume participation, borrow costs
Validation	PBO and Deflated Sharpe
The key decision is not which strategy has the highest backtested CAGR. It is which one produces the highest credible net return after robustness and cost gates.
Keep RNG-001 archived and unchanged so the new results remain clearly independent from the previously rejected hypothesis.
